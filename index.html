Our Mission

The LGBTQ+ Project is dedicated to safeguarding and celebrating LGBTQ+ history. As a digital repository, we preserve artifacts, stories, and pivotal moments from our past, ensuring they remain accessible for future generations. By keeping our history alive online, we strive to educate, inspire, and empower individuals worldwide.

The Digital Archives, in partnership with Archive.org, present a comprehensive repository of LGBTQ+ history, offering you access to an extensive collection of digital content right at your fingertips. Dive into a wealth of historical documents, articles, photographs, videos, and more that have shaped the LGBTQ+ community over the decades.

Explore pivotal moments in history with keywords like Stonewall Riots, Harvey Milk, Lavender Scare, ACT UP, Marsha P. Johnson, and many others. Whether you’re researching activism, cultural milestones, or personal stories that have left an indelible mark on LGBTQ+ heritage, this archive serves as your gateway to preserving and understanding the past.

Begin your search now and help keep LGBTQ+ history alive and accessible for generations to come.
